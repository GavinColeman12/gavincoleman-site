## The problem it solves

Every SMB service practice lives or dies on its online reputation — Google reviews, Yelp stars, Healthgrades ratings, state bar profiles. But most practices treat reputation as a periodic crisis rather than an ongoing system. The case study embedded above shows what a full Crescendo reputation audit looks like end-to-end, using an anonymized composite of a real law firm engagement. It walks through the data gathering, the sentiment signals, the response-rate analysis, the competitor benchmarking, and the prioritized recommendations that came out of the actual engagement.

## Who it's for

Dental groups, law firms, medspas, and multi-location professional services practices where online reviews meaningfully drive new-client volume. Especially valuable for practices with fragmented review surfaces (multiple platforms, multiple office locations) that are hard to track manually.

## What it does under the hood

A multi-platform review ingestor pulls reviews from Google Business Profile, Yelp, Healthgrades, Avvo, and industry-specific platforms into a unified schema. Claude runs sentiment analysis and theme extraction on the full review corpus. A scoring engine weights recency, platform authority, and sentiment to produce a composite reputation score. The output is a single 40-page audit report with prioritized recommendations, competitor benchmarking against three peer practices, and an implementation roadmap.

## Engagement structure

Two weeks from kickoff to first full audit. Week one — connect the client's review platforms and competitor identification. Week two — run the audit, review findings with the client, and hand over the action plan. Ongoing monthly monitoring ($400–$800 per month) available for clients who want continuous tracking with weekly anomaly alerts.
