# Website Rewrite Plan — Crescendo Consultants

## The core problem

The current site reads generic because it does two things that every other "independent AI consultant" site also does:

1. **Lists what you do** (AI strategy, automation, chatbots) instead of **why anyone should hire _you_ specifically**
2. **Understates your credibility** — "5+ years in consulting" and a four-logo strip (Walmart, Visa, BoA, Publicis Sapient) reads like a smart junior, not a senior operator. A prospect's first thought is "why not just hire Accenture?"

The fix isn't more adjectives. It's reframing the whole narrative around **the specific pattern library you've built across industries at the Fortune 100 tier**, and letting that credibility do the heavy lifting. The site should make a mid-market CEO feel like they're getting a quiet steal — Bain/Publicis Sapient-caliber thinking without the agency layer.

---

## Positioning thesis — what you *actually* bring

Strip away the generics and this is the differentiated pitch:

> **You've been the person at Bain and Publicis Sapient who helps Fortune 100 CEOs turn AI and data into real P&L outcomes — across banking, retail, hospitality, entertainment, and fintech. You've seen the playbooks at the scale most consultants will never touch. Now you deliver that same playbook to mid-market operators directly, for a fraction of what a big firm charges, and you write the code yourself.**

Four pillars that make this non-generic:

| Pillar | What it means | Why it matters to the buyer |
|---|---|---|
| **Fortune 100 pattern library** | Hands-on AI/data work at Santander, Amex, Walmart, Marriott, Disney, DSG, etc. | They're buying cross-industry synthesis — you've seen what works at scale in 6+ verticals |
| **Strategy + shipped code** | You present to the C-suite in the morning and push production code that afternoon | Most consultants hand off a deck. You hand off working systems |
| **Financial discipline** | Trained in advisory/M&A — every rec justified by unit economics | You don't pitch AI for AI's sake. You build the business case |
| **Direct partnership** | No account managers, no SOW theater, no offshore ticket queue | They get the senior person on every call, every deliverable |

**Tagline candidates** (pick one, we'll A/B):
- *"Bain-grade strategy. Built by the person who ships the code."*
- *"Fortune 100 AI playbooks, delivered directly to mid-market operators."*
- *"The senior consultant that actually builds the thing."*
- *"Strategy from Bain. Code that ships. No agency layer."*

Recommendation: **lead with option 2** (credibility-forward) and use option 1 as a supporting subhead.
